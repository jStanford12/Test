Population Distribution

The West Midlands County is the region's population anchor, home to nearly 3 million people. The surrounding shires are significantly more sparsely populated, highlighting the concentration of residents in the urban core.

The Urban Engine: A Deep Dive into the West Midlands County

A "Super-Diverse" Metropolis

The county is the second most ethnically diverse region in England, a characteristic that defines its social fabric. The Asian community forms the largest minority group, followed by the Black community.

A Youthful, Challenged Economy

Reflecting its young population, retirement is low. However, the county faces higher unemployment and economic inactivity due to long-term sickness compared to the national average, indicating structural economic challenges.

The Rural Heartlands: An Aging Population

The Silver Shires: A Generational Shift

The surrounding shires are aging significantly faster than the urban core and the national average. Over a quarter of Shropshire's population is now over 65, posing long-term challenges for healthcare and the local workforce.

Shire Economies: A Profile

The economies of the shires show distinct profiles shaped by their rural character. High rates of retirement and self-employment are common, with very low unemployment. Herefordshire stands out for its high self-employment, likely tied to agriculture.

Housing & Lifestyle Differences

Lifestyle and living situations vary dramatically. Car dependency is high in the shires, while home ownership is the norm. In the urban West Midlands, lower car ownership and higher rates of renting reflect a different economic and social reality.

The Future: Challenges & Opportunities

The demographic trajectories of the two Midlands create distinct strategic challenges. The shires must manage an aging population and potential labor shortages, while the urban core must focus on closing the skills gap and addressing deprivation to unlock the potential of its young, diverse population.
